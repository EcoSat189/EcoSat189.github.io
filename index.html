The Growing Space Debris Problem

500,000+ Debris Pieces

Over half a million pieces of space junk orbit Earth, traveling at speeds up to 17,500 mph. Even tiny fragments can destroy satellites and endanger astronauts.

Kessler Syndrome Risk

Collisions create more debris, potentially triggering a cascade effect that could render low Earth orbit unusable for generations.

Reentry Pollution

Most debris eventually falls back to Earth, often burning incompletely and releasing harmful particles into the atmosphere.

Our Sustainable Solution

Autonomous Debris Collection

EcoSat uses AI-powered cameras and proximity sensors to identify, approach, and capture space debris with minimal energy expenditure.

Green Propulsion

Solar-powered electric propulsion enables movement without chemical fuels, reducing space pollution.

Controlled Eco-Reentry

When full, EcoSat executes a controlled atmospheric reentry at optimal angles to maximize debris vaporization, minimizing ecological impact.

The Teen Scientist Tackling Space Junk One CubeSat at a Time

How 17-year-old Karina Faria Guimarães is revolutionizing space sustainability with her EcoSat project

Featured in TechTomorrow • may 2025

In São Paulo, Brazil, a revolution in space sustainability is taking shape. 17-year-old Karina Faria Guimarães holds up a 15cm cube that represents a potential solution to one of space exploration's biggest challenges: orbital debris.

Why Space Debris Matters

With over 500,000 pieces of space junk larger than 1cm orbiting Earth at speeds up to 17,500 mph, even tiny fragments can disable satellites or endanger astronauts. The International Space Station regularly maneuvers to avoid collisions, and the growing debris field threatens future space missions.

From Project to Space Solution

What began as a science fair project evolved into EcoSat - a self-contained debris removal system that fits in a standard CubeSat format. The ingenious design combines:

  • Organic photovoltaic solar panels for sustainable power
  • AI-powered debris identification and tracking
  • Electrostatic capture mechanisms to secure debris
  • Controlled atmospheric reentry that maximizes vaporization
"Most people think you need billions and NASA to solve space problems. EcoSat proves sustainable solutions can be small, smart, and accessible."

The Breakthrough Technology

At EcoSat's core is its innovative capture system. Unlike proposals that would require large nets or robotic arms, Karina's design uses modified electrostatic forces to attract and secure debris without physical contact. This reduces both the system's complexity and the risk of creating additional fragments.

"The real innovation," explains Karina, "is in the complete lifecycle approach. Most proposals focus only on capture, but we've designed the entire process from identification through safe disposal."
